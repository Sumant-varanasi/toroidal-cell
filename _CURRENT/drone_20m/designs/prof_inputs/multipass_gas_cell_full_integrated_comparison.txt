Full Integrated Tolerance, Drift, Cost and Material Comparison for Concave-Mirror Multipass Gas Cells
Laboratory, field and drone applications, including aluminium, metals, Bosch rail, plastic and hybrid mirror-mount designs
Prepared as a first-order engineering tolerance budget. Values are maximum expected engineering estimates and should be validated by CAD, FEA, thermal cycling and vibration testing.
[Heading 1] Executive summary
This version fully integrates plastic designs into every major comparison section, rather than treating plastic as an add-on. Construction, alignment, operational drift, total tolerance, cost and recommendations are now evaluated for metal, aluminium, Bosch rail, plastic and hybrid options.
Construction tolerance is manufacturing/fixing accuracy. It is a capture-range and build-feasibility check. If construction error can be corrected by alignment, it is not directly added to final in-use drift.
Alignment tolerance is the residual six-degree error left after the user aligns the system. It depends on the component resolution, ease of alignment, visibility of the spot pattern, locking method and user capability.
Operational drift is the post-alignment change under thermal, vibration, pressure and cycling loads. This is the dominant final tolerance for field and drone operation.
Theta means independent input-angle drift. It is reported separately from spot-position drift. Spot-position drift is Pxy in mm. Angular drift is Theta_xy in mrad. For estimating internal spot movement at the second mirror, the angle term contributes L x Theta_xy, which is 0.3 x Theta_xy for L = 300 mm.
The best drone technical design is machined aluminium or titanium/Invar local insert with an isostatic flexure. The best low-cost drone design is a hybrid plastic body with an aluminium mirror cartridge. All-plastic mounts are attractive for cost and weight but require a relaxed spot-pattern tolerance envelope.

Term | Definition | Meaning

[Heading 1] 4. Alignment tolerance: component resolution, ease and user capability
Alignment tolerance is the residual left after alignment. It is not the same as construction tolerance. A mount may be manufactured with large error but still align well if capture range and adjustment resolution are adequate. Conversely, a low-cost plastic pocket may be accurately made but hard to align because it has little or no post-assembly adjustment.
--- TABLE ---
Regime | Meaning | dx mm | dy mm | dz mm | dθx mrad | dθy mrad | dθz mrad | Residual Pxy mm | Residual Theta_xy mrad | Equivalent combined spot mm
Easy | High-visibility alignment, accessible kinematic screws/flexures, iterative spot-pattern observation, expert or trained user. | ±0.03 | ±0.03 | ±0.05 | ±0.05 | ±0.05 | ±0.5 | 0.042 | 0.071 | 0.064
Medium | Limited access, lockable cartridge or jig alignment, trained technician, output coupling not fully visible. | ±0.08 | ±0.08 | ±0.12 | ±0.15 | ±0.15 | ±1.0 | 0.113 | 0.212 | 0.177
Hard | Monolithic or fixed mount, poor visibility, small aperture/spot clearance, production operator or non-adjustable plastic reference. | ±0.18 | ±0.18 | ±0.25 | ±0.40 | ±0.40 | ±2.0 | 0.255 | 0.566 | 0.424
--- END TABLE ---
Practical interpretation: Easy alignment corresponds to a laboratory-style optical setup where the input/output beam and spot pattern can be viewed and iteratively tuned. Medium alignment corresponds to a lockable cartridge or jig-aligned field/drone assembly. Hard alignment corresponds to restricted access, fixed plastic references, monolithic detector placement or an operator with limited feedback.

--- TABLE ---
Rank category | Design | Reason

[Heading 1] 12. Engineering notes for next design iteration
If a plastic route is selected, use metal threaded inserts, dowel/boss references, a local aluminium or stainless mirror seat, and avoid using the O-ring as the mirror-plane reference.
For a dense Herriott spot pattern, design the mirror support such that combined spot variation is comfortably below the available clearance between beam spots and mirror hole. For drone, the aluminium flexure or hybrid plastic + aluminium cartridge should be tested first.
For the final design, run FEA for modal frequency and thermal deformation, then validate with thermal cycling, pressure cycling and vibration testing. The first resonance of the mirror cartridge should ideally be above the main drone vibration band.
The document uses conservative maximum addition. If measured data are available, replace the maximum-add model with a statistical RSS model and measured drift coefficients.
